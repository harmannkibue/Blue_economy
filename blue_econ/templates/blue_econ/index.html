WHAT IS BLUE ECONOMY?

It is the overall contribution of the oceans to economies,the need to address the environmental and ecological sustainability of the oceans, and the oceans economy as a growth opportunity for both developed and developing countries. Secretary-General Patricia Scotland: “The Blue Charter will help countries develop an integrated approach to the building of the blue economy, one which considers the value of often overlooked sectors such as artisanal fishing as well as the role of women and young people.”

-Source:The centre for the blue economy

-Source:http://thecommonwealth.org/blue-economy

Aims of the Blue Economy

  1. Building a sustainable and eco-friendly economy in East Africa.
  2. Creating awareness on 'blue' economy. This will help empower people from all walks of life and also help in the conservative measures of the environment emulating countries such as Finland but applied to a larger scale.
  3. Aims at the improvement of human well being and social equity,while significantly reducing environmental risks and ecological scarcities similar to 'Green Economy'.

In accordance with the blue economy finance principles blue economy also aims at:

  1. Promote the implementation of the Sustainable Development Goals(SDGs).
  2. Set out ocean specific standards while avoiding the duplicate of existing frameworks for responsive invesment.
  3. Comply with IFC performance standards and EIB environmental and social principles and standards.
